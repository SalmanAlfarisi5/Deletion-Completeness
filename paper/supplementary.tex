This supplement reports the granular numbers behind the headline results in the main
paper. Section references (\S) point to the corresponding subsection of the main text.
All adversary rates are over the four-reasoner panel: \texttt{gpt-4o-mini},
\texttt{gpt-4o}, Claude~Sonnet~5, and GPT-5.5. The completeness threshold is
$\tau=0.10$ throughout. Datasets: 253 isolated, 298 multi-hop, 963 context, 250
parametric-gradient.

\section{Residual Survival and Duplication (main paper \S Residual Survival)}

Naive single-record deletion leaves the value present in $97.2\%$ of isolated PII targets
(Wilson $[94.4, 98.7]$; exp01, $n{=}253$). Artifact-aware deletion drives this to $0\%$
($[0, 1.5]$; exp02). A $2{\times}2$ factorial over embedder (MiniLM / OpenAI) $\times$
injection cadence ($0$\,s / $1.5$\,s) shows value duplication in all four cells (row
inflation $+174$--$182\%$); over corpus-unique values the incidence is $80$--$82\%$, with
both byte-identical and paraphrase-variant duplicates present in every cell (neither
uniformly dominant, unlike the earlier smaller-scale run). The mechanism is independently
documented in Mem0 issues \#4896 (hash-only dedup), \#4573, and \#687 (byte-identical
write race).
