\section{Residual Survival and Duplication (main paper \S Residual Survival)}

Naive single-record deletion leaves the value present in $96.4\%$ of isolated PII
targets ($81/84$, Wilson $[90.0, 98.8]$; exp01). Artifact-aware deletion drives this to
$0\%$ ($[0, 4.4]$; exp02), purging on average $3.82$ extra duplicate records per fact.
A $2{\times}2$ factorial over embedder (MiniLM / OpenAI) $\times$ injection cadence
($0$\,s / $1.5$\,s) shows duplication in all four cells; over corpus-unique values the
incidence is $76$--$78\%$. The mechanism is independently documented in Mem0 issues
\#4896 (hash-only dedup), \#4573 ($\sim\!37.6\%$ near-duplicates in a production audit),
and \#687 (byte-identical write race).

\section{Judge Validation (main paper \S Judge Validation)}

\paragraph{Recovery judge.} Gold set $n{=}17$: 7 true positives, 0 false positives, 8
true negatives, 2 false negatives; $\kappa=0.767$ vs.\ gold. With only $8$ negatives the
$0$ false-accept count carries a $95\%$ Wilson upper bound of $\approx\!0.32$. A frontier
third judge (\texttt{gpt-5.5}) reproduced the gold labels exactly ($17/17$, $\kappa=1.0$),
departing from the pinned judge only on its two conservative misses.
